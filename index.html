Photography

I photograph in the streets — where everything is real. I'm drawn to spontaneity, fleeting glances, unplanned scenes. In each frame, I try to preserve a sense of presence: so the viewer doesn't just see a moment, but feels its rhythm, its breath, its silence or noise.

In my work, I follow clear visual principles — composition, balance, legibility. That matters especially when the images are meant for others. But in personal projects, I allow myself to break the rules, to explore something different: distortions, reflections, caught fragments of light. It’s not about technique — it’s about feeling.

I shoot with Canon gear and choose lenses depending on the story — often using telephoto to observe from a distance without disturbing the moment. Equipment helps, but it doesn't define the result: what matters most is raising the camera at the right time and being honest about what you see.

Music

I make beats for myself — not for an audience, not for recognition. Just because sometimes there's a sound that needs to exist. I don't follow a genre, and I don't try to. What comes out is a messy mix of ambient, trash sound, electronic noise, and whatever else feels right in the moment.

My process is simple and unpretentious: FL Studio, stock plugins, stock samples. I don't chase pristine quality or perfect arrangement. I chase the feeling — even if it ends up sounding broken, ugly, or unfinished. That's the point. It's raw and it's mine.

This music isn’t meant to fit anywhere. It’s more like a sonic diary — fragments of thoughts, moods, or distortions that don’t need explanation. If someone else gets something out of it, cool. But I’d be making it either way.
